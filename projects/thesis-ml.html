B.S. Thesis — ML for Sleep Data

Making wearable sleep data transparent, calibrated, and actionable. Building evidence-constrained models with human-readable feedback.

  • Ridge
  • XGBoost
  • Calibration
  • Wearables

2024

Advisor: Prof. Arielle Baskin-Sommers
Dept. of Psychology, Yale

I'm building this thesis to turn sleep signals into guidance people can trust. Instead of chasing leaderboard scores, I'm focusing on calibrated models and an explainer constrained to peer-reviewed sleep science. The goal is product-minded rigor: transparent features, reliable feedback, and recommendations that feel usable in real life.

Project Approach

  • Train calibrated Ridge + XGBoost on multi-night wearable + survey features; prefer reliability over raw scores.
  • Two-layer design: models for accuracy + an explainer constrained to peer-reviewed sleep science.
  • Validate against self-reports and cognitive tasks to check real-world relevance.
  • Emphasize transparent features and feedback loops so people understand why a suggestion was made.

Semester Milestones

Data ready

Clean/align nights, engineer ~100–120 features, audit leakage.

Baselines + calibration

Ridge + XGBoost; reliability curves (ECE/Brier) and thresholds.

Validation

Compare to self-reports & cognitive tasks; subgroup error analysis.

Constrained explainer

LLM narratives bound to vetted sleep sources with safety checks.

Success Signals

  • Well-calibrated predictions (e.g., ECE < 0.05) with clear calibration plots.
  • Advisor-reviewed feature attributions that align with sleep science.
  • Users can accurately restate "why" behind a suggestion in brief tests.
  • No safety violations or off-source claims in the explainer.
